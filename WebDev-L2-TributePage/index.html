About Her

Kalpana Chawla was an Indian-American astronaut and aerospace engineer who inspired millions by breaking barriers in space science.

Early Life

  • Born: 17 March 1962, Karnal, Haryana (India)
  • Grew up in a simple middle-class family
  • Developed interest in flying and aircraft models at a young age
  • Often visited local flying clubs and airports to learn about airplanes
  • Excelled in science and mathematics during school
  • Was one of the few girls pursuing technical education in her region
  • Dreamed of becoming an aeronautical engineer from childhood

Education

  • Schooling: Tagore School, Karnal
  • Bachelor’s: Aeronautical Engineering – Punjab Engineering College
  • Master’s: Aerospace Engineering – University of Texas, Arlington
  • Ph.D: Aerospace Engineering – University of Colorado Boulder
  • Specialized in fluid dynamics and aircraft propulsion systems
  • Published research in aerodynamics and space engineering

Journey to NASA

  • 1988: Began research career in aerospace engineering in the USA
  • Worked at NASA research centers before becoming astronaut candidate
  • 1994: Selected by NASA as an astronaut trainee
  • Completed intense astronaut training including survival & technical simulation
  • Trained in robotics, spacecraft systems, and mission operations
  • 1997: First space flight aboard Space Shuttle Columbia (STS-87)
  • Became the first Indian-born woman in space

Space Missions

  • STS-87 (1997): First mission in space
  • Studied solar physics and microgravity effects
  • STS-107 (2003): Final mission (16-day scientific research)
  • Conducted 80+ experiments in space science
  • Studied materials science, Earth observation, and biology
  • Helped improve understanding of life in microgravity conditions

Achievements

🚀 First Indian-born woman in space
🛰️ 2 NASA space missions
🔬 80+ space experiments
🌍 Global inspiration
